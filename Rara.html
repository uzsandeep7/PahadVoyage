Rara Lake – The Queen of Himalayan Lakes

Rara Lake, often hailed as the "Queen of Himalayan Lakes," is Nepal’s largest and deepest freshwater lake, nestled within the remote Mugu District of Karnali Province. It is a pristine destination offering breathtaking scenery, tranquil waters, and a sense of serenity.

Itinerary

Day 1: Kathmandu to Nepalgunj

Fly from Kathmandu to Nepalgunj. Explore the bustling town near the Indian border.

Day 2: Nepalgunj to Jumla – Trek to Chere Chaur

Early morning flight to Jumla, then begin trek to Chere Chaur (3–4 hours).

Day 3: Chere Chaur to Chalachaur

Trek through beautiful meadows and forests to reach Chalachaur (2,980 m) — approx. 5–6 hours.

Day 4: Chalachaur to Sinja Valley

Descend to Sinja Valley (2,490 m), the ancient capital of the Khas Kingdom.

Day 5: Sinja Valley to Ghorosingha

Trek along the Hima River, ascending through forests and ridges to Ghorosingha (3,050 m).

Day 6: Ghorosingha to Rara Lake

Final stretch of the trek to Rara Lake (2,990 m) — approx. 4–5 hours. Enjoy views of the pristine lake surrounded by pine forests and mountains.

Day 7: Explore Rara Lake

Spend the day relaxing or exploring the scenic lake surroundings. Take photographs, journal, or interact with locals.

Day 8: Rara Lake to Talcha – Fly to Nepalgunj and Kathmandu

Trek to Talcha Airport and take return flights to Nepalgunj and then to Kathmandu.

Important Details:

  • Days: 8
  • Difficulty: 4 of 5 (Strenuous)
  • Min Group Size: 1
  • Max Group Size: 14
  • Trek Region: Rara Region
  • Type of Trek: Private Trip
  • Highest Elevation: 3,050 m (10,007 ft)
  • Best Season: Spring (March-May) & Autumn (Sept-Nov)
