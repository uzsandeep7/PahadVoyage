Rara Lake – The Queen of Himalayan Lakes

Rara Lake, often hailed as the "Queen of Himalayan Lakes," is Nepal’s largest and deepest freshwater lake, nestled within the remote Mugu District of Karnali Province. It is a pristine destination offering breathtaking scenery, tranquil waters, and a sense of serenity.

Itinerary

  • Day 1: Fly from Kathmandu to Nepalgunj. Explore the bustling town near the Indian border.
  • Day 2: Nepalgunj to Jumla (Flight) – Trek to Chere Chaur. Early morning flight to Jumla. Begin trek to Chere Chaur (3–4 hrs).
  • Day 3: Chere Chaur to Chalachaur (2,980m). Trekking through beautiful meadows and forests (5–6 hrs).
  • Day 4: Chalachaur to Sinja Valley (2,490m). Trek downhill to Sinja, an ancient capital of the Khas Kingdom.
  • Day 5: Sinja Valley to Ghorosingha (3,050m). Ascend along the Hima River, crossing forests and ridges.
  • Day 6: Ghorosingha to Rara Lake (2,990m). Final trek to Rara Lake (4–5 hrs). Enjoy views of the turquoise lake surrounded by pine forests and mountains.
  • Day 7: Explore Rara Lake. Relax or explore the area around the lake. Photograph, journal, or interact with locals.
  • Day 8: Rara Lake to Talcha Airport – Fly to Nepalgunj and then Kathmandu.

Important Details:

  • Days: 8
  • Difficulty: 4 of 5 (Strenuous)
  • Min Group Size: 1
  • Max Group Size: 14
  • Trek Region: Rara Region
  • Type of Trek: Private Trip
  • Highest Elevation: 3,050 m (10,007 ft)
  • Best Season: Spring (March-May) & Autumn (Sept-Nov)
  • Transportation: Domestic Flight & Private Vehicle
